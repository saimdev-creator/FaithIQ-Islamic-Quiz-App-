01. Pillars of Islam
How many pillars of Islam are there?
Answer: 5
(True) Islam is built on five pillars: Shahada, Salah, Zakat, Sawm, and Hajj.
Which is the first pillar of Islam?
Answer: Shahada
(True) The Shahada (Testimony of Faith) is the first and most fundamental pillar.
How many daily prayers are obligatory?
Answer: 5
(True) Muslims are required to perform five daily prayers: Fajr, Dhuhr, Asr, Maghrib, and Isha.
Which pillar involves fasting?
Answer: Sawm
(True) Sawm refers to fasting during the daylight hours of the month of Ramadan.
Hajj is performed in which city?
Answer: Makkah
(True) Hajj is the pilgrimage to the Holy Kaaba located in Makkah.
Which pillar of Islam requires a Muslim to declare faith in Allah and His Messenger?
Answer: Shahada
(True) The Shahada is the testimony that there is no god but Allah and that Muhammad ﷺ is His Messenger.
Which pillar of Islam requires giving a portion of one’s wealth to the needy?
Answer: Zakat
(True) Zakat is the obligatory charity given to purify one’s wealth and support those in need.
How many times is Hajj required in a Muslim’s lifetime if they are able?
Answer: Once
(True) Hajj is required once in a lifetime for those who are physically and financially able.
Which pillar most directly builds daily discipline through regular worship?
Answer: Salah
(True) Salah, the five daily prayers, builds discipline and keeps a Muslim connected to Allah throughout the day.
Fasting in Ramadan mainly teaches which of the following?
Answer: Patience and self-control
(True) Sawm teaches patience, self-control, empathy for the poor, and closeness to Allah.
02. Prophets in Islam
Who is the last Prophet in Islam?
Answer: Prophet Muhammad ﷺ
(True) Prophet Muhammad ﷺ is known as Khatam an-Nabiyyin (The Seal of the Prophets).
Which Prophet built the Kaaba?
Answer: Prophet Ibrahim (AS)
(True) Prophet Ibrahim (AS) and his son Isma'il (AS) rebuilt the Kaaba together.
Which Prophet was swallowed by a whale?
Answer: Prophet Yunus (AS)
(True) Prophet Yunus (AS) was swallowed by a large fish/whale after leaving his people.
Which Prophet received the Torah?
Answer: Prophet Musa (AS)
(True) The Torah (Tawrat) was the holy book revealed by Allah to Prophet Musa (AS).
Who was the first Prophet?
Answer: Prophet Adam (AS)
(True) Prophet Adam (AS) was the first man created and the first Prophet of Islam.
Which Prophet built a large ark by Allah’s command to save the believers from a great flood?
Answer: Prophet Nuh (AS)
(True) Prophet Nuh (AS) built the ark and called his people to worship Allah alone before the flood.
Which Prophet was known for his remarkable patience during severe trials?
Answer: Prophet Ayyub (AS)
(True) Prophet Ayyub (AS) remained patient and grateful to Allah despite losing health, wealth, and family.
Which Prophet interpreted dreams and became a minister in Egypt?
Answer: Prophet Yusuf (AS)
(True) Prophet Yusuf (AS) was blessed with the ability to interpret dreams and later became a leader in Egypt.
Which Prophet spoke directly with Allah on Mount Sinai and received commandments?
Answer: Prophet Musa (AS)
(True) Prophet Musa (AS) was spoken to directly by Allah and received the commandments on Mount Sinai.
Which Prophet was born without a father by the command of Allah?
Answer: Prophet Isa (AS)
(True) Prophet Isa (AS) was born miraculously to Maryam (AS) without a father by Allah’s command.
03. Quran Knowledge
How many Surahs are in the Quran?
Answer: 114
(True) The Holy Quran contains 114 Surahs, starting with Al-Fatiha and ending with An-Nas.
Which is the first Surah of the Quran?
Answer: Al-Fatiha
(True) Surah Al-Fatiha is the opening chapter and is recited in every unit of prayer.
Which is the longest Surah in the Quran?
Answer: Al-Baqarah
(True) Surah Al-Baqarah is the longest chapter with 286 verses.
In which month was the Quran revealed?
Answer: Ramadan
(True) The Quran was first revealed to Prophet Muhammad ﷺ during the month of Ramadan.
Which Surah is known as the heart of the Quran?
Answer: Yaseen
(True) Surah Yaseen is often called the “Heart of the Quran” based on various traditions.
In which language was the Quran originally revealed?
Answer: Arabic
(True) The Quran was revealed in clear Arabic (lisanan arabiyyan mubina) to Prophet Muhammad ﷺ.
What is the name of the night in which the first revelation of the Quran came down?
Answer: Laylat al-Qadr
(True) The first revelation came on Laylat al-Qadr (Night of Decree) in the month of Ramadan.
Which Surah contains Ayat al-Kursi (The Verse of the Throne)?
Answer: Surah Al-Baqarah
(True) Ayat al-Kursi is verse 255 of Surah Al-Baqarah, describing Allah’s majesty and knowledge.
Approximately how many years did it take for the Quran to be revealed completely?
Answer: 23 years
(True) The Quran was revealed gradually over about 23 years, guiding the believers step by step.
What is the main theme of Surah Al-Ikhlas?
Answer: Unity of Allah (Tawhid)
(True) Surah Al-Ikhlas emphasizes the oneness of Allah and rejects all forms of shirk (associating partners with Him).
